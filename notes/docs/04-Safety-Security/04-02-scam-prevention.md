## 1. Policy Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes procedures for identifying, preventing, and responding to scams, exploitation, and malicious activities targeting community members.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all community platforms, events, and activities, and covers all forms of member exploitation and fraudulent behavior.

### 1.3 Objectives
- Protect members from financial and personal harm
- Maintain community integrity and trust
- Provide clear reporting and response procedures
- Educate members on scam identification and prevention

## 2. Scam Classification

### 2.1 Job and Recruitment Scams

#### 2.1.1 Fake Job Postings
**Red Flags:**
- Unrealistic salary promises
- Requests for upfront payment or fees
- Vague job descriptions
- No company website or verifiable information
- Pressure to make quick decisions
- Requests for personal financial information
- "Wishy-washy" communication or unclear requirements

#### 2.1.2 Unpaid Work Exploitation
**Red Flags:**
- "Portfolio building" opportunities that benefit only the employer
- Requests for extensive work before payment discussion
- Vague promises of future payment or equity
- Pressure to work for "exposure" or "experience"

### 2.2 Investment and Financial Scams

#### 2.2.1 Cryptocurrency and Investment Schemes
**Red Flags:**
- Guaranteed high returns
- Pressure to invest quickly
- Requests for cryptocurrency payments
- Pyramid or MLM structures
- Unregistered investment opportunities

#### 2.2.2 Identity Theft and Credential Harvesting
**Red Flags:**
- Requests for personal identification documents
- Phishing attempts for login credentials
- Fake verification processes
- Requests for bank account information

### 2.3 Technical Exploitation

#### 2.3.1 Malware and Phishing
**Red Flags:**
- Suspicious links or downloads
- Requests to install unknown software
- Fake security alerts or updates
- Requests for remote access to devices

#### 2.3.2 Code and Intellectual Property Theft
**Red Flags:**
- Requests for complete source code without proper contracts
- Vague project descriptions
- Pressure to share proprietary information
- Requests for work samples that could be used commercially

### 3.1 Education and Awareness

#### 3.1.1 Regular Education Campaigns
- Monthly scam awareness posts
- Quarterly workshops on recognizing scams
- Annual security best practices training
- Case studies of common scam patterns

#### 3.1.2 Resource Library
- Scam identification guides
- Safe job search practices
- Investment and financial literacy resources
- Technical security best practices

### 3.2 Verification Processes

#### 3.2.1 Job Opportunity Verification
**Required Information:**
- Company name and website
- Contact person with verifiable LinkedIn profile
- Clear job description and requirements
- Salary range and benefits information
- Interview process details

#### 3.2.2 Investment Opportunity Verification
**Required Information:**
- Registered investment advisor information
- SEC or relevant regulatory registration
- Clear risk disclosures
- Audited financial statements
- Independent third-party verification

### 3.3 Platform Security

#### 3.3.1 Communication Monitoring
- Automated detection of suspicious patterns
- Keyword filtering for common scam terms
- Link scanning for malicious content
- Regular security audits

#### 3.3.2 Member Verification
- Email verification for new members
- Optional identity verification for certain activities
- Regular review of member activity patterns
- Reporting system for suspicious behavior

## 4. Reporting Procedures

### 4.1 How to Report Scams

#### 4.1.1 Immediate Reporting
- **Emergency Contact:** community@ai-engineers.org
- **Core Team:** community@ai-engineers.org
- **Anonymous Reporting:** [ANONYMOUS_FORM]

#### 4.1.2 Report Requirements
**Include in Reports:**
- Date and time of incident
- Description of the scam or suspicious activity
- Screenshots or evidence
- Names or usernames of involved parties
- Any financial losses or damages
- Contact information for follow-up

### 4.2 Reporting Process

#### 4.2.1 Response Timeline
1. **Immediate Action:** Report as soon as possible
2. **Evidence Collection:** Gather screenshots, messages, and other evidence
3. **Core Team Review:** Investigation within 24 hours
4. **Member Protection:** Immediate protection measures if needed
5. **Follow-up:** Regular updates on investigation progress

---

## 5. Response Procedures

### 5.1 Immediate Response

#### 5.1.1 For Active Scams
- Remove scammer from all community channels
- Warn other members about the threat
- Preserve evidence for investigation
- Provide support to affected members
- Report to relevant authorities if necessary

#### 5.1.2 For Affected Members
- Immediate support and guidance
- Connection to appropriate resources
- Documentation of losses or damages
- Assistance with recovery efforts
- Ongoing support as needed

### 5.2 Investigation Process

#### 5.2.1 Investigation Steps
1. Evidence collection and preservation
2. Pattern analysis for similar incidents
3. External verification with other communities or authorities
4. Member interviews with affected and witness members
5. Comprehensive incident report creation

#### 5.2.2 Follow-up Actions
**Community Protection:**
- Update security measures
- Enhance education and awareness
- Improve verification processes
- Strengthen reporting mechanisms

**Member Support:**
- Provide resources for recovery
- Connect with legal or financial advisors
- Offer emotional support and counseling
- Facilitate connections with other affected members

## 6. Member Protection Guidelines

### 6.1 Safe Practices

#### 6.1.1 Job Searching
- Research companies thoroughly before applying
- Never pay fees for job applications or interviews
- Verify job postings through official company channels
- Be cautious of remote work opportunities
- Trust your instincts about suspicious offers

#### 6.1.2 Financial Decisions
- Never invest based on community recommendations alone
- Verify all investment opportunities independently
- Be skeptical of guaranteed returns
- Consult with licensed financial advisors
- Never share financial information with strangers

#### 6.1.3 Technical Security
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Be cautious of suspicious links or downloads
- Keep software and systems updated
- Use reputable security software

### 6.2 Warning Signs

#### 6.2.1 Communication Red Flags
- Pressure to act quickly
- Requests for secrecy or confidentiality
- Vague or evasive answers to questions
- Inconsistent information or stories
- Requests for personal or financial information

#### 6.2.2 Opportunity Red Flags
- Promises that seem too good to be true
- Requests for upfront payment or investment
- Lack of verifiable information
- Pressure to recruit others
- Requests for work without proper contracts
